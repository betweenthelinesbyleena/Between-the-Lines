Between the Odds

Loving you was never a constant,
But always a variable, changing—
With you, becoming for you,
And in the journey beside you,
I found myself: the real me.

I first saw you as a penguin,
With a cute little walk
And a lovely small bag;
But your shoulders told a different story.
You were the kind of penguin
That made me fall even harder.

Then I saw you as a monkey—
Not one caged by thin bars,
But one imprisoned within my heart.
My chaotic kid, my best friend, my healer.
You were the kind of monkey
That merged with my soul.

And then, I lost you within me.
I searched, but I couldn't find you.
You were there, just existing,
And I was there, figuring things out,
Unable to do anything
Other than loving you as myself.

After that, I saw you annoyed by me—
By all the little things—
And I knew then that I had lost you completely.
But I know you love the graceful me,
And not the vexing one;
That is where I loved you from a distance,
Completely shattered.

Now, here we are, standing beside one another,
With so many differences and goals,
And much less compatibility than we thought.
But somewhere, between all the odds,
I love you more than I imagined,
And I hope you will love me,
Maybe, just a little bit.
